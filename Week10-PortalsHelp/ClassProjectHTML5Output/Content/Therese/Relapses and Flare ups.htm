Relapses and Flare-ups

Relapses and flare-ups happen, but no one likes them.  The goal is to recognize the warning signs and take the necessary steps to prevent, minimize, or shorten it.  Several tools can help, including the following:

  • Understand the warning signs of a relapse, and take action if any arise.

  • Speak with your health practitioner about any concerns or issues.

  • Continue to stay active with your social support network.

  • Strategize ways to anticipate and avoid situations and activities that might spark a relapse or flare-up.

  • Minimize anger, anxiety, stress or tension as much as possible.

  • Prioritize activities that promote a positive, balanced, healthy and creative lifestyle.

  • Always take action if the overall recovery starts to feel stuck or sidetracked.

These types of steps can help prevent or minimize a relapse.
